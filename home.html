What is Generative AI?


      Generative AI, also known as GenAI, is artifical intelligence that creates content such as images, videos, text, stories and music using the user's input.
      It emerged in the late 2010s from AI models like Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs), and is derived from the broader concept of AI, which exists on chatbots and smart assistants like Alexa, Bixby, and Siri.
      GenAI has turned into a popular topic since the release of ChatGPT in 2022, providing benefits for individuals and organizations, despite showing risks and difficult challenges.

How does Generative AI work?

  • It starts with a foundation model (FM) which that seves as the basis of multiple GenAI applications.
  • Then, the GenAI starts tuning to transfer data of the FM to a specific application.
  • Large language models (LLMs) are a subset of FMs, like ChatGPT's generative pre-trained transformer (GPT).
  • GPT-3 has the ability to generate content in one of the billion parameters from just little input.
  • After generating content, the GenAI waits for evaluation from the user, and retunes its algorithm improving quality and accuracy.
